A Discussion On Health

Why Veganism?

Kidney diseases

Eating less animal-based foods can lower the acid load and put less stress on your kidneys. Plant-based foods that are not highly processed, such as fresh vegetables and whole grains, have phytates.
Obesity

A well-planned vegan diet is typically lower in saturated fat and cholesterol, improving heart health and reducing risk of cardiovascular diseases. Also vegan diets tend to be rich in fiber, which aids in digestion.

Heart diseases

The results, published in the European Heart Journal, showed vegetarian and vegan diets cut bad cholesterol by 10%, cut total cholesterol by 7% and cut apolipoprotein B (the main protein in bad cholesterol) by 14%.
Inflammation and arthritis

A vegan diet provides a wealth of vitamins, minerals, antioxidants and phytochemicals that support a robust immune system, reduce inflammation and protect against chronic diseases such as type 2 diabetes.
Some Comrade Vegan Recipes

Dive In The Kitchen

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice, rinsed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 3 tsp Pilau Masala or 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until golden brown.
  2. Add the carrots, bell pepper, and green peas. Cook for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Stir in the ground cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cinnamon stick. Cook for another minute.
  4. Add the rinsed rice and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed, about 15-20 minutes.
  5. Fluff the rice with a fork and season with salt to taste before serving.
  6. Can be served with Beans, Lentils or Greengrams curry

Ingredients

  • 1 cup lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Add the spices (cumin, coriander, turmeric, curry powder) and cook for another minute.
  3. Stir in the lentils, coconut milk, vegetable broth, and tomatoes. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until lentils are tender, about 20-25 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve the lentil curry over cooked coconut rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch collard greens, washed and chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a sufuria skillet, sauté the onion and garlic until golden brown.
  2. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they begin to soften.
  3. Stir in the ground cumin, coriander, and chili flakes (if using). Cook for another minute.
  4. Add the chopped Sukuma wiki to the sufuria and cook until wilted and tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot as a side dish or with Ugali, Rice or a Starch of your choice
  7. Add Avocado if in season
